Health &
Nutrition

It’s well-established that milk and dairy are an important part of a healthy diet. In fact, they’re among some of the most nutrient-rich foods available. And Ontario dairy farmers, along with the entire Canadian dairy system, are committed to ensuring the milk you drink meets the highest standards of purity and quality.

15
Essential nutrients

Milk is packed with vitamins and other nutrients. Some you’ve probably heard of – others you might not know. Here’s a quick rundown of what they are, and some of the things they help your body do.

Vitamin A

This multitasking nutrient is important to bone and tooth development, the maintenance of night vision and healthy skin. It also plays a role in cell division and immunity, and has anti-oxidant properties.

Vitamin B12

Milk and dairy products are an excellent source of this vitamin that’s essential for neurological function and building redblood cells.

Vitamin D

Your body can create this important nutrient when it’s exposed to sunlight – though probably not quite as much as you need. Top up with some milk or dairy products to enhance your calcium and phosphorus absorption for strong bones and teeth.

Calcium

Most of us know that calcium helps build and maintain healthy bones and teeth. There aren’t many foods that have as much calcium as dairy products.

Magnesium

Like calcium, magnesium also helps keep your bones and teeth healthy. It also plays a role in helping your body convert food into energy.

Protein

Dairy products contain high quality protein that helps build and repair bones and other body tissues. It also helps your body build antibodies to fight infection.

Phosphorus

An essential mineral that’s needed by every cell in your body, phosphorus helps your body build and maintain healthy bones and teeth.

Pantothenic acid

This helps convert food into energy and builds bones and other tissues.

Dairy and your health

In round figures

A 200 ml glass of milk contains about the same amount of protein as one egg.

Choose your flavour

Whether you like to drink 1%, 2%, homogenized or chocolate milk, it all contains the same nutritional elements.

Heart healthy

A recent study suggests that dairy consumption was associated with a 10% lower risk of cardiovascular disease and a 12% lower risk of stroke.

Test your nutrition knowledge

The nutritional facts are clear, but there are still a lot of misconceptions about dairy products. Can you filter out the truth from the falsehoods?

True or False?

You can enjoy dairy if you’relactose intolerant

False

Warm milk can be a pleasant, comforting drink before you head off to bed – and it certainly counts toward your recommended number of servings. But while it does contain the sleep-inducing amino acid Tryptophan, it doesn’t have a high enough quantity to induce sleepiness.

Pasteurized milk is healthier than raw milk

True! Pasteurization is the process of heating milk at temperatures high enough to kill potentially harmful bacteria. It not only makes your milk safe to drink, it helps extend its shelf life by destroying organisms that can make it spoil sooner.

dairynutrition.ca

Milk can contain antibiotics

False! Sometimes cows get sick – but strict precautions are taken to ensure the milk you drink doesn’t contain any medication they may be taking. The farmer will continue to milk her, but the milk is discarded for a mandatory period until the medication is no longer in her system.
